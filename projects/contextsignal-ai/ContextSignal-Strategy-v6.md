## 1. Why v6 Exists

Strategy v5.2.2 was built on three assumptions that turned out to be wrong:

| Assumption | Reality |
|---|---|
| 5–7 hrs/week available | 2–3 hrs/week |
| AI-drafted, human-edited content (30–60 min per post) | Claude does nearly all writing — minimal human editing |
| Domain expertise to add original screenshots, workflows, opinions | No direct PM or sales tool experience — content is research-based |

These constraints make an SEO-first, volume-driven strategy unviable:

- **Google penalises AI content without genuine E-E-A-T signals.** Without original screenshots, personal workflows, or first-hand opinions, editorial content looks like every other AI-generated guide.
- **The time budget can't support editorial review at scale.** At 2–3 hrs/week, reviewing even 4–5 posts means spending 60% of time on editing alone — leaving nothing for distribution, products, or admin.
- **Volume without quality is a liability.** Publishing 50+ posts/month that are indistinguishable from ChatGPT output risks the domain, doesn't build an audience, and won't convert.

**The pivot:** From "SEO content site with AI help" to "AI-powered digital product business with content as the funnel."

### What Stays from v5.2.2

Not everything changes. These elements carry forward:

- Audience-vertical organising principle (roles, not tools)
- WordPress on Kinsta + GeneratePress tech stack
- Beehiiv for email with custom sending domain
- Pretty Links + UTM conventions
- GA4 + Search Console instrumentation
- Editorial policy (what cannot be claimed, QA checklist)
- Content templates (workflow guide, money page formats)
- Brand terms (AI PM OS, ContextSignal Score)
- Trader compliance framework (for when that vertical launches)
- Stop rules (adapted for lower volume — see Section 8)

### What's Gone

| v5.2.2 Element | Status | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 automated content pipeline | **Removed** | Without human editing or domain expertise, automated posts are pure AI filler — exactly what Google is devaluing |
| Make.com automation | **Removed** | No automation pipeline needed without Tier 1 |
| 8 verticals across 3 waves | **Cut to 1** | At 2–3 hrs/week, spreading across 2+ verticals means neither gets enough depth |
| 3-tier content model | **Simplified to 2 tiers** | Standard (weekly) + Flagship (monthly) |
| 500+ posts in 12 months | **Cut to ~55** | Quality over volume |
| $5k–$10k/month at Month 12 | **Cut to $1k–$2k** | Honest projection given constraints |
| Signature framework share kits | **Removed** | Over-engineering for a pre-revenue site |
| Framework definition pages | **Removed** | Same — build these when there's traffic to justify them |
| Content tracker spreadsheet | **Removed** | Not needed at 4–5 posts/month |
| Product waitlists | **Removed** | Just build the product |
| 5-email welcome sequences | **Simplified to 3** | Shorter, more focused |
| Partnership outreach (10 messages/week) | **Deferred** | No authority to leverage yet — revisit at Month 3 |

## 2. Revenue Model

**Core insight:** Digital products sell on utility — buyers care if the prompts work, not whether the creator personally uses Notion daily. This is where Claude-generated content is genuinely strong.

### Revenue Mix

| Revenue Stream | Weight | Monthly Est. (Month 12) | Google Dependent? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Digital products (prompt packs, templates, toolkits) | **45%** | $450–$900 | No — email list + social |
| Affiliate commissions | 20% | $200–$400 | Partially — search + email |
| Newsletter sponsorships | 20% | $200–$400 | No — direct audience |
| Display advertising | 5% | $50–$100 | Yes — pageview dependent |
| Community / consulting | 10% | $100–$200 | No — relationship-based |

**Total target (Month 12):** $1,000–$2,000/month.

### Why Products First

- **No E-E-A-T dependency.** A prompt pack doesn't need the author to prove they've used Notion for 3 years. It needs to work.
- **Claude's strength.** Generating high-quality prompts is literally what Claude does best. The product quality will be genuinely high.
- **Content becomes product samples.** Every blog post showcases 2–3 prompts from the pack, with a CTA to get the full set. Content has a clear purpose from day 1.
- **Email list is the distribution channel.** Products sell to the email list, not to Google searchers. This removes Google dependency from the core revenue stream.

## 3. One Vertical: Project Managers

Start with PM only. Add Sales only after PM is generating revenue.

### Why PM First

- Most straightforward product angle (prompt packs, workflow templates)
- Clearest tool affiliates (Notion, Monday.com, ClickUp — all with strong programs)
- Large, identifiable audience on Reddit, LinkedIn, and professional communities
- PM workflows are structured and repeatable — perfect for AI-generated templates

### PM Vertical Definition

**Positioning:** *AI workflow templates that turn PM busywork — docs, updates, risk tracking — into a 30-minute weekly system.*

**Target audience:** Product managers, project managers, Scrum Masters, PMO leads, operations managers.

**Core topics:** AI-powered sprint planning, automated status reports, AI for stakeholder communication, prompt templates for project documentation, risk assessment with AI, meeting notes automation, retrospective facilitation.

**Canonical tool stack:**
1. **Notion** — AI-powered docs, sprint boards, knowledge base
2. **Monday.com** or **ClickUp** — project tracking with AI automations
3. **Slack** — team communication (AI summaries, workflow triggers)
4. **Google Workspace** — docs, sheets, slides with Gemini AI
5. **Otter.ai** or **Fireflies** — meeting transcription and action items
6. **Zapier** — workflow automation connecting the stack

**Key affiliate programs:** Notion ($8–10/referral + recurring), Monday.com (25% recurring), ClickUp (20% recurring), Asana, Linear.

### When to Add Sales

**Gate:** PM vertical is generating $200+/month from products + affiliates combined, with 300+ email subscribers.

**Sales vertical spec:** Carried forward from v5.2.2 Section 4 (AI for Sales Professionals) — same positioning, tool stack, and affiliate programs. Adapt to the v6 content model (2 tiers, product-first) when the time comes.

## 4. The Product: AI Prompt Templates for PMs

The product is both the lead magnet AND the revenue generator.

### Free Version (Lead Magnet)

**"50 AI Prompt Templates for Project Managers"**

A PDF with ready-to-use prompts organised by PM workflow:
- Sprint planning (8 prompts)
- Status reports & updates (8 prompts)
- Stakeholder communication (8 prompts)
- Risk assessment (6 prompts)
- Meeting facilitation (6 prompts)
- Retrospectives (6 prompts)
- Documentation (8 prompts)

**Production:** Claude generates all prompts. Ale reviews for coherence and quality. Formatted as a clean PDF.

**Distribution:** Gated behind email signup on every blog post + hub page.

### Paid Version

**"The Complete AI PM Toolkit — 150+ Prompt Templates & Workflow Guides"**

Everything in the free version, plus:
- 100+ additional prompts across all categories
- Workflow guides showing how to chain prompts together
- Tool-specific versions (Notion, Monday.com, ClickUp variants)
- Quarterly updates with new prompts

**Price:** $29–$39 (test both price points)

**Platform:** Gumroad or LemonSqueezy (no monthly cost, transaction fees only).

**Launch target:** Month 2–3, once 300+ email subscribers and 10+ blog posts are live.

### Content-Product Loop

Every blog post is a sample of the product:

> "How to Use AI for Sprint Retrospectives" → includes 3 prompts from the pack → inline CTA: "These are 3 of 50 free PM prompt templates. Get the full pack →"

This means every post has a clear purpose — it's not just hoping for SEO traffic. The content drives product awareness, and the product drives email signups.

## 5. Two-Tier Content Model

| Tier | Cadence | Production | Purpose | Revenue Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard | 1/week | Claude-generated, quick scan by Ale (10–15 min) | Workflow guides targeting long-tail keywords. Funnel to lead magnet. | Email list growth, contextual affiliate links |
| Flagship | 1/month | Claude-generated, more thorough review by Ale (30–45 min) | Comprehensive guides that anchor the vertical. Funnel to digital product. | Product conversion, link-worthy content, newsletter feature |

**Total: ~4–5 posts/month.** Not 50.

### Standard Posts

**Format:** Workflow guide (template from v5.2.2 Section 10).
- Title: "How to Use AI for [Specific Task] as a [PM Role]"
- Structure: Problem (2 sentences) → Workflow (step-by-step with prompts) → What this replaces → CTA
- Length: 1,000–1,500 words
- Includes: 2–3 prompts from the product pack, vertical CTA, internal link to hub page
- Review time: 10–15 minutes (scan for errors, verify links, publish)

**Quality standard:** These won't have original screenshots or personal opinions. They will be well-structured, accurate, and useful. Accept that they're AI-generated and optimise for utility rather than pretending otherwise.

### Flagship Posts

**Format:** Comprehensive guide or money page.
- Title: "The Complete Guide to AI for [PM Task] (2026)" or "Best AI Tools for [PM Task] 2026"
- Structure: Varies — but always includes a downloadable asset, multiple prompts, and tool recommendations
- Length: 2,000–3,000 words
- Includes: 5+ prompts, comparison elements, CTA to paid product, internal links to 3+ related posts
- Review time: 30–45 minutes (more careful read, verify claims, check links)

### What's NOT in the Content Model

- No automated content pipeline
- No news/updates tier
- No "prompt tip of the day" posts
- No tool directory pages (not enough traffic to justify)
- No framework definition pages (build when there's demand)

## 6. Distribution: Newsletter First, SEO as Bonus

With AI-generated content and no E-E-A-T advantages, Google organic will be slow and unreliable. Don't depend on it.

### Primary Channels

| Channel | Approach | Time/Week |
|---|---|---|
| **Newsletter** (Beehiiv) | Biweekly. Curate best content + 1 exclusive tip + product CTA. Direct inbox delivery, no Google dependency. | 20 min avg |
| **Community engagement** (Reddit, LinkedIn) | Value-first posts in r/projectmanagement, PM LinkedIn groups. Answer questions, share tips. Link to content only when genuinely relevant. 3:1 ratio rule (3 no-link contributions per 1 with link). | 30 min |
| **SEO** | Optimise posts with Rank Math. Submit sitemap to Search Console. Target long-tail keywords. But don't build the business plan around it. | Built into publishing |

### Newsletter Strategy

**One newsletter:** "The Context Signal" — biweekly, PM-focused (expand when Sales launches).

**Format:**
- 1 featured post (link to latest blog)
- 1 quick tip (exclusive to newsletter — not on the blog)
- 1 product mention (CTA to free pack or paid product)
- Total: 300–500 words. Quick to read, quick to write.

**Welcome sequence (3 emails):**

1. **Email 1 (Day 0) — Deliver + connect:** Deliver the lead magnet. "Reply and tell me what AI tool you're using for PM work." (Reply prompt trains inbox providers.)
2. **Email 2 (Day 3) — Best workflow tip:** One high-value workflow from the blog. Pure value, no pitch.
3. **Email 3 (Day 7) — Product intro:** "The free pack has 50 prompts. The full toolkit has 150+ with workflow guides. Here's what's inside →"

### What's NOT in Distribution

- No partnership outreach in Month 1 (no authority to leverage yet)
- No Pinterest strategy
- No repurposing pipeline (carousels, threads, etc.) — revisit when there's content worth repurposing
- No paid distribution

## 8. Stop Rules (Adapted for v6)

These prevent waste. If a metric isn't tracking, the response is "diagnose and fix" — never "publish more."

| Trigger | Response |
|---|---|
| Month 1: fewer than 30 email signups | Stop publishing. Fix lead magnet offer, CTA placement, distribution. The product may not be compelling enough — test different angles. |
| Month 3: fewer than 100 subscribers | Rewrite lead magnet. Increase community engagement (daily 10-min Reddit/LinkedIn sessions for 2 weeks). Test CTA copy. |
| Posts getting traffic but no signups | CTA is invisible or irrelevant. Test inline CTA placement (not just end-of-post). Test different lead magnet headlines. |
| Email signups happening but open rates below 25% | Deliverability audit: check DKIM/SPF/DMARC via mail-tester.com. Review subject lines. Suppress unengaged (90+ days). |
| Month 3: 100+ subscribers but zero product sales | Tighten the lead magnet → product bridge. Make Email 3 clearer. Test pricing ($29 vs $19). Survey subscribers: "What would make this worth buying?" |
| Affiliate links getting clicks but no conversions | Review the tool's landing page. Check cookie duration. Consider whether the recommendation is genuinely strong. |

### A/B Testing Rule

From v5.2.2 — still applies:
- Test one variable at a time
- Minimum sample: 200 pageviews or 2 weeks
- Keep a "winning CTA" log (simple text file)

## 16. Editorial Policy

Carried forward from v5.2.2 Section 10, adapted for 2 tiers.

### What Cannot Be Claimed

- No tool is "the best" without specifying context and criteria
- No pricing without "last verified" date
- No affiliate commission figures in public content
- No claims about AI replacing professionals — "augmenting" and "assisting"
- No fabricated reviews, fake quotes, or invented data
- No unverified statistics

### QA Checklist (Before Publishing Any Post)

1. Title clearly identifies the role AND the task?
2. At least 2 prompts from the product pack included?
3. Pricing/feature claims tagged with "last verified: [date]"?
4. Affiliate links contextual and natural?
5. Lead magnet CTA present?
6. Internal link to hub page present?
7. Meta description written and under 155 characters?
8. Correct URL slug under PM hub?

### Content Templates

Carried forward from v5.2.2:

**Workflow Guide (standard posts):** Problem → Workflow (step-by-step with prompts) → What this replaces → CTA. 1,000–1,500 words.

**Money Page (flagship):** Quick verdict → Criteria → Tool breakdown → Comparison table → Recommendation → CTA. 2,000–3,000 words.

## 18. Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google doesn't rank AI-generated content | High | Medium | Newsletter-first distribution. SEO is a bonus, not the plan. |
| Low email signups (lead magnet doesn't resonate) | Medium | High | Stop rule at Month 1 (30 signups). Test different angles. |
| Product doesn't sell | Medium | High | Survey subscribers. Test pricing. Iterate on content. |
| Niche competition from established PM sites | Medium | Medium | Focus on prompt utility (hard to compete with) not authority (easy to lose). |
| Affiliate program rejections | Medium | Low | Publish without links, add later. Products are primary revenue anyway. |
| Burnout from content grind | Low | Medium | Only 4–5 posts/month. If it's not fun, slow down — this is a side project. |
| Google algorithm update | Medium | Low | Low exposure — only 5% of revenue model depends on display ads/SEO. |

## 20. What This Strategy Doesn't Do

Being explicit about trade-offs:

- **Doesn't build SEO authority fast.** Accepting this. Newsletter and community are the distribution plan.
- **Doesn't scale to 8 verticals in 12 months.** Accepting this. Depth > breadth with limited time.
- **Doesn't produce content that passes as human-written.** Accepting this. Optimising for utility (prompts that work) not authenticity (personal stories).
- **Doesn't hit $5k+/month in Year 1.** Accepting this. $1k–$2k is realistic and enough to validate the model.
- **Doesn't automate content production.** Accepting this. Automation without editing is a liability.
